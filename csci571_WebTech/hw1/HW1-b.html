The Top 10 Most Famous Supernovae in History

  • SN 185: This "guest star" was observed by Chinese astronomers in the Book of Later Han , and might have been recorded in Roman literature. It remained visible in the night sky for eight months. This is believed to be the first supernova for which records exist.

  • 1. SN 1006: This Type Ia supernova, observed in 1006 AD, was the brightest recorded stellar event in history. Visible worldwide and brighter than Venus, it could even be seen during the day for weeks.

  • 2. SN 1054 (Crab Nebula): Observed in 1054 AD by Chinese astronomers, this supernova is responsible for creating the Crab Nebula, a prominent supernova remnant. It was incredibly bright, remaining visible during the day for nearly a month and outshining everything but the Moon. Its remnant includes a rapidly spinning neutron star, or pulsar, at its center.
